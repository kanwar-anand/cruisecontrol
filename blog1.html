5 Reasons Commuters are Bored with Radio

Chris Hann | 17July2017

Commuters are bored of radio. The age-old, go-to option of in-car entertainment is dying as commuters are increasingly looking to more productive ways of spending that morning and evening commute. This blog post explores 5 reasons why commuters are leaving radio due to boredom:

  1. 1. Bad, bad games

    You know the games we’re talking about..listen to this poorly recorded sound for half a second and guess what is is. After all, who doesn’t know the difference between the sound of someone crumpling paper, reaching into a bag of chips, or turning the page of a newspaper.

  2. 2. Idiotic callers

    The guy calls in to play a classic gambler’s game, take the money or take another chance at winning more. It seems the only people that call in for these games have no concept of strategy or goals. Come on people, go in with a number in mind of what you want to achieve, it saves us having to listen to you agonize over the same decision over and over again.

    Then there’s the callers into political talk shows. Not only does Johnny have no idea of the complexities of US-North Korea relations, he also seems to think dropping a nuclear bomb is the first thing a President should do to a whole nation of deprived and innocent civilians.

  3. 3. No balance between music, intelligence, and sport

    If you’re like me, you have your 3 presets programmed into your radio:

    1. There’s your music station with genre of choice, be it pop, country, rock, or classical.

    2. You have your talk radio station when you’re feeling in the mood to engage your brain on the commute to or from work.

    3. Then there’s your sports station where you get to the most opinionated of all radio hosts go over the big plays or trades from the night before.

    The question is this: where is the NBC version of radio stations, a one-stop shop for all your entertainment needs? Everything in moderation would save a lot of preset button pressing to fulfil your itch for the other interests in your life.

  4. 4. Same songs over, and over, and over again

    Did you hear that Ed Sheeran / Adele / Rascal Flatts have new singles out? Of course you have! Why? Because your music radio station kindly played it for you once on the way to work and once on the way home, every day for the last 6 weeks.

    You take that quick trip to the grocery store on the weekend, and what is playing but that same song…Happy by Pharell Williams...AGAIN! We can’t take any more Happy, Pharell…let’s be sad, anxious, angry, anything but that again.

  5. 5. Commercials that belong in the ‘80s

    If we were to get a copy of the guide to radio advertising as sent out by today’s radio stations to prospective advertisers, it would surely read like this:

    1. Go to your local library and check out the book published in 1983 by Edna Bloggs on how to produce a hip radio commercial

    2. Buy a microphone and a subscription to sounds with titles such as “crowd applause” and “crowd groans”

    3. Pick from your extensive 4-person staff to find out which one always wanted to be an actor but never followed their dream. Tell them they’re not talented enough and proceed to hire yourself for the role.

    4. Record 30 seconds of cheese that would convince only your mother to come purchase your services and goods

  6. Alternatives?

    Commuters are bored of radio. That’s why they are increasingly turning to their own music playlists, podcasts, and even silent meditation. Commuters are not mere lemmings to be entertained, they are often successful, driven, young professionals who want nothing more than to turn the commute into productivity. This trend is giving rise to companies such as CruiseControl whose mission is to turn the daily commute productive time.
